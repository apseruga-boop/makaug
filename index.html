What is a Mortgage?

A mortgage is a long-term loan used to buy property. You pay a deposit upfront, and the bank finances the balance. You then repay monthly with interest over an agreed period.

  • Deposit: Your upfront contribution (usually 15%-40%).
  • LTV: Loan-to-Value. Higher LTV means lower deposit, but often stricter qualification.
  • Term: How long you repay (e.g. 10 to 25 years).

Mortgage Terms Explained

Interest Rate

The annual cost charged by the bank on the outstanding loan. Even small rate differences can significantly change monthly repayments.

Amortization

How your monthly payment is split between interest and principal over time. Early payments are usually more interest-heavy.

Arrangement Fee

A one-time setup fee charged by the bank, often a percentage of the approved loan amount.

Early Repayment

Some lenders charge a fee if you clear the loan early. Always check this before signing your offer letter.

Our Mission

MakaUg.com exists to modernize Uganda's property marketplace by improving listing quality, reducing fraud risk, and making verified property information easier to access for buyers, renters, investors, and agents.

We combine local market knowledge with practical digital tools: map-based search, district-level filters, and WhatsApp-assisted listing for users who prefer mobile workflows.

Our goal is simple: make it easier to move from enquiry to verified viewing, and from verified viewing to informed decision-making.

What We Provide

  • Property discovery for sale, rent, student housing, commercial units, and land.
  • Direct contact with owners or AREA-verified agents.
  • Clear listing status indicators (private listing vs agent listing).
  • Structured listing forms designed around Ugandan market realities.
  • Support channels via phone, email, and WhatsApp.

Trust and Compliance Focus

  • Identity verification processes for listers and broker profile validation.
  • Fraud reporting workflow with review and escalation.
  • Privacy and data-handling controls aligned with Ugandan legal requirements.
  • Safety guidance for inspections, payments, and documentation checks.
  • Internal review controls before publishing sensitive listing changes.

Uganda Market Context

Uganda's property market includes multiple tenure and transaction structures. Users should always conduct due diligence, including title verification, contract review, and lawful payment trails. For regulated steps such as stamp duty assessment, registration, and title processing, always confirm current requirements with the relevant government authorities.

MakaUg.com is a listing and discovery platform. We do not act as legal counsel, escrow provider, or party to sale or tenancy contracts.
